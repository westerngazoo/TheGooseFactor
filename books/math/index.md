# Math Basics

Concise, high-impact notes on the mathematics behind algorithms, analysis, and systems design. Definitions first, then intuition, then examples, then connections to code.

## What's Covered

### 1. Discrete Math Refresh
Sets, logic, relations, functions, natural numbers, ordering, cardinality — the type system for reasoning about algorithms.

### 2. Real Numbers & Precalculus
The real number system (field axioms, order, completeness), algebra refresher (inequalities, polynomials), and trigonometry — everything needed before Apostol's Calculus.

### 3. Calculus
Limits, continuity, differentiation, and integration — following Apostol's rigorous approach with an emphasis on why the definitions work.

### 4. Modular Arithmetic
Congruences and the arithmetic rules of clock math; the Euclidean algorithm, GCD, and modular inverses; fast (binary) exponentiation; and the Chinese Remainder Theorem with its applications. The algebra behind hashing, checksums, and cryptography.

### 5. Combinatorics
Counting principles (permutations and combinations), binomial coefficients and Pascal's triangle, stars and bars with inclusion–exclusion, and the pigeonhole principle alongside generating functions — counting without listing.

### 6. Probability
Sample spaces, events, and the axioms; conditional probability and Bayes' theorem; random variables, expectation, and variance (with linearity of expectation); common discrete distributions; and tail bounds — Markov, Chebyshev, and Chernoff — for high-probability guarantees.

### 7. Graph Theory
Graph definitions and representations; connectivity, trees, and shortest paths; cuts and the max-flow/min-cut theorem; and bipartite matching with graph coloring — the structural core of graph algorithms.

### 8. Linear Algebra
Vectors, matrices, and linear maps; linear independence, basis, and rank; the four fundamental subspaces; projections and least squares; and determinants and eigenvalues — the spectral glimpse where linear algebra pays off.

## Approach
- **Minimal fluff**: definitions, intuition, canonical examples.
- **Bridge to code**: every concept connects to an algorithmic technique.
- **Goose personas** debate the hard parts so you don't have to wonder alone.
