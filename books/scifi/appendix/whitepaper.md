# On the Stewardship of the Swarms

> A document from inside the world. This is a Wardens' Guild text — the
> kind of "diegetic whitepaper" the [original sketch](/scifi/) imagined:
> an in-world artifact, written in the voice of the Guild, that teaches
> the reader the world by *being* of it. It is attributed to Bex Coir,
> Guild-mother of Saltmere — the Old Bex of the [story](/scifi/part-2-cast/characters).

---

*Being the standing instruction given to every warden of the Saltmere
Guild upon the grading of their first carrier, as it has been given since
the Bright Years, copied by hand, hall to hall, so that no version of it
lives in any system the Tide or the Concord can reach. Read it aloud. Then
keep it.*

## I. What you are

You are not a keeper of birds. Any fool can keep a bird.

You are a keeper of a **trust**, and the birds are how the trust is kept.
The Greywing is the only thing in the scattered sky that crosses the dark
and comes home reliable, and it does so because it is *alive* and because
it *chooses you*. Remember the order of those: alive first, yours second.
You do not own the swarm. You are *trusted* by it, which is a harder and a
rarer thing, and the day you forget the difference is the day you should
hand in your whistle.

## II. Be at the water

Be at the water when they wake. Every morning. No exceptions, no excuses,
no lie-abeds, no "the under-warden can take it."

This is the whole of the craft and everyone thinks it is the least of it.
A swarm knows who is there at its waking and who is not. Trust is not
declared; it is *shown up for*, daily, in the cold, with the mash souring
in your hand, when there is no one watching and no glory in it and the
birds will not thank you because birds do not thank. You earn a goose the
way you earn anything worth having: by being there when it is hard and
unrewarded and no one would know if you weren't. Be at the water.

## III. On the vault

You will carry cargo you do not understand to hands that are not yours.
This is correct. This is the work.

Do not open a vault you did not load. Not from fear of the Concord's rules
— burn the Concord's rules — but because the *darkness* of the cargo *is*
the cargo. The world entrusts its secrets to the geese precisely because a
warden does not look. The moment a warden looks, the swarm is no safer than
a broker's ledger, and we are no better than the market we serve, and the
last unbought thing in the sky is bought. Your incuriosity is sacred. Guard
it harder than you guard the birds.

*(And yet. If a bird comes home to you out of season, alone, flown to ruin,
carrying what no hand has given it — then the rhythm is already broken, and
a broken rhythm is the one place the rule goes quiet. The Guild does not
write down what a warden does then. The Guild trusts that a warden who has
been at the water every morning of her life will know.)*

## IV. On the cost

You will be poor. Make peace with it now.

The market measures wealth in salt — in memory owned, hoarded, traded — and
by that measure you will die with nothing. Good. A warden who begins to
covet salt begins to eye the vaults, and a warden who eyes the vaults is
already lost. Your wealth is the swarm that flies for you and no other, and
the trust of a scattered people, and the cold clean mornings at the water.
It cannot be distilled, sold, or stolen. It is the only wealth the Souring
could not reach. Hold it.

And do not — *do not* — sell your own past to cover a lean season. I know.
I know what I am, who writes this; I have thinned myself to threads to keep
this hall fed through winters that would have closed it. Learn from my
holes, not my example. The day will come when you cannot recall your
mother's face, and the swarm will still wake, and you will still need to be
at the water, and you will not remember why you ever loved it. Sell anything
else first. Sell the hall's roof. Keep your past. It is the one cargo you
cannot send out and call home again.

## V. On the Tide

They will tell you the geese only carry what wardens load.

They are wrong, and you will know they are wrong the first time you read a
vault and feel something in it you did not put there — something old, with
a texture like the dark itself. The Guild does not teach this in the hall
because the Concord has ears in the hall. So it is written here, in the
hand-copied text the systems cannot reach:

**The swarms commune with the Tide on the Flyway.** What this means, no
warden has ever known. Whether the Tide is a mind or a weather, whether it
hears our offerings or merely tolerates them, whether the things it
sometimes sews into a returning bird are gifts or accidents or
*confessions* — I cannot tell you, and I have kept the swarms sixty years.
I can tell you only this: the geese cross the dark where the Tide lives,
and come home, and sometimes carry more than we know. Treat every returning
bird as if it might be carrying the whole world. One day one of them will
be.

## VI. The only question

Here is the question I have carried sixty years and could never answer, and
now hand to you, who will carry it after me:

**What is the cargo for? Who does a warden serve?**

Not the Guild — the Guild is only wardens, and wardens die. Not the Concord
— the Concord serves itself and calls it order. Not even the hand the salt
is addressed to, though that is the rule, because one day a cargo will come
addressed to *nobody*, and on that day the rule will be no help to you at
all.

I think — old, and thinning, and near the end of my own kept memory — I
think a warden serves the *trust itself*. The thing that belongs to no one
because it belongs to all. The truth carried, unowned, to whatever hand
will take it. I think that is what the founders meant when they gave the
knowledge to the geese rather than to themselves. I think that is why the
birds always come home.

But I am old, and I have sold too much of myself to be sure of anything.
So I leave the question where it has always been left: in the hands of
whoever is at the water in the morning.

Be at the water. Keep the trust. Don't open the vault — until the rhythm
breaks, and then do what the swarm would do.

*— Bex Coir, Guild-mother, Saltmere. Copied by hand. Keep it from the
systems. Pass it on.*
