# Character Briefs

> The people of *Flyway* — who they are, what they want, and what they're
> hiding. Read these before the novella, or after, or not at all; the
> story stands without them, but they deepen it. Light spoilers for
> *roles*, none for *outcomes*.

## Issa Marsh — the warden

**Role:** protagonist; flyway-warden of Saltmere
([Factions & Places](/scifi/part-1-world/factions-and-places)).

Issa is in her late thirties, marsh-born, a warden since she could walk —
the third generation of her family to tend the Saltmere swarms. She's
quiet, stubborn, more comfortable with geese than people, and carries a
warden's particular poverty: rich in trust, poor in salt. She has never
left Saltmere and never wanted to.

- **Wants:** to keep her swarm whole, to be left alone, to do the work
  her mother and grandmother did. A small life, well kept.
- **Hides:** that she's begun to believe the heresies — that the geese
  carry more than cargo, that the Tide *speaks* on the Flyway. She's never
  said it aloud. She's afraid it's true.
- **Arc:** the out-of-season goose forces her off Saltmere, into the
  markets she despises and the questions she's feared, and asks her to
  decide what a warden's trust is *for* when the whole world wants what
  she's carrying.

## Ledger — the goose

**Role:** the out-of-season Greywing; the inciting mystery; the most
important character who never speaks.

**Ledger** is one of Issa's swarm — an old gander, cantankerous even by
goose standards, named (warden humor) for his habit of *remembering debts*
(he bites those who've wronged him, sometimes years later). He returns to
Saltmere alone, out of season, ragged, and carrying in his crop-vault a
salt that no one gave him — a memory that should not exist
([The Greywings](/scifi/part-1-world/the-greywings)).

- **Wants:** (as much as a goose wants) to come home, to be fed, to bite
  Halvard Crane.
- **Carries:** the whole plot. The memory in Ledger is the thing everyone
  reaches for; keeping Ledger *alive* is the thing that keeps Issa in the
  story (kill the bird, lose the cargo).
- **Note:** Ledger is written as a real goose — no telepathy, no speeches.
  His "decisions" are exactly as legible and as opaque as a real animal's.
  Whether he's an instrument of the Tide or just a stubborn old bird who
  flew home is a question the book keeps open, and Issa keeps asking.

## Halvard Crane — the broker

**Role:** principal antagonist; senior broker of the Concord, based at
Cinder Reef.

Crane is urbane, genuinely intelligent, and not a sadist — he's something
worse for the story: a *true believer in the system*. He thinks the
memory market is civilization's great achievement (it preserved knowledge
through the Souring; it gives the poor *something* to sell), and he
defends it the way a good banker defends the bank. He wants Ledger's salt
verified, controlled, and — if it threatens the market's trust — *erased*.

- **Wants:** to control the uncontrollable salt; to keep the Concord's
  monopoly on truth intact; to be, in his own eyes, the reasonable one.
- **Hides:** that he knows whose memory it is — and that the Concord's
  founding has a crime distilled into founder-salt
  ([Memory & Salt](/scifi/part-1-world/memory-and-salt)) that Ledger is
  carrying home.
- **Foil:** Crane is Issa's opposite — distilled, controlled, dead
  memory against living, kept, untradeable trust. He is what the world
  becomes if the market wins.

## Sefa Roon — the Reader

**Role:** wildcard; a Tide-priest (Reader) of the deep shrine at Cinder
Reef ([Factions & Places](/scifi/part-1-world/factions-and-places)).

Sefa reads the Tide — and unlike most Readers, she may actually be right.
She's spent decades at the shrine built into a still-thrumming Old World
core, and she's convinced the Tide has begun, after a century of silence,
to **return something**. When she hears of an out-of-season goose
carrying a memory that smells of the machine-weather, she knows what it
is before Issa does.

- **Wants:** to understand what the Tide is doing — and she's not sure
  whether that means protecting the memory or surrendering it to the
  shrine.
- **Hides:** how much she *doesn't* know; she performs certainty she
  doesn't feel. And she's afraid the Tide is not returning a gift but
  *confessing* something.
- **Role in plot:** the one who can tell Issa what she's carrying — and
  whose own agenda (worship? understanding? sacrifice?) makes her an
  unreliable ally.

## Marek Tarn — the salvager

**Role:** irregular ally; captain of a salvage crew working the dark
between Saltmere and Cinder Reef.

Marek is the story's frontier — rough, funny, superstitious about the
Tide (he lives *in* the dark it inhabits), and connected to everyone
because he goes everywhere outside the Concord's reach. He owes Issa
(she nursed a sick swarm that once saved his crew's lives with a warning),
and that debt is why he'll carry a warden and a wanted goose to Cinder
Reef with no questions logged.

- **Wants:** to pay his debt and get clear; to not get killed for someone
  else's secret. Pragmatic, not heroic — which makes his choices matter.
- **Provides:** the off-the-books path between habitats, the salvager's-eye
  view of the dark and the Tide, and a running argument that the heroic
  thing and the smart thing are rarely the same.

## Old Bex — the Guild-mother

**Role:** mentor; eldest warden of Saltmere, head of the Guild-hall.

Bex raised Issa into the work and is the keeper of the Guild's oldest
lore — including the heresies, which she neither confirms nor denies. She's
the voice of *the trust*: what a warden owes the geese, the habitats, and
the dead. She's also dying, slowly, having sold too much of her own memory
in lean years — a living example of the market's cost
([Memory & Salt](/scifi/part-1-world/memory-and-salt)).

- **Wants:** to pass on the trust before her own past runs out; to see
  Issa become the warden she could be.
- **Gives:** the question Issa carries through the book — *"What's the
  cargo for, girl? Who does a warden serve?"* — and, eventually, the cost
  of answering it.

> :happygoose: A cast note: nobody here is a chosen one. Issa is a
> mid-career bird-keeper who'd rather be home. Marek is paying off a
> favor. Crane thinks *he's* the reasonable adult in the room — and from
> inside his life, he is. Even Ledger is just a grumpy old goose who flew
> home. The story happens *to* ordinary people doing their jobs, which is
> the only kind of story this world tells. The geese don't need heroes.
> They need someone to show up and keep the trust. That turns out to be
> harder, and braver, than heroics.

## The shape of the cast

The cast maps onto the factions ([Factions & Places](/scifi/part-1-world/factions-and-places)),
so the people *are* the conflict:

- **Issa + Bex + Ledger** — the Wardens' Guild: living trust.
- **Crane** — the Concord: distilled, controlled memory.
- **Sefa** — the Readers: the Tide, and the question of what it wants.
- **Marek** — the salvagers: the path between, and pragmatism's argument.

Six figures, four factions, one out-of-season goose. Now the story.
