# Timeline

> How the world of *Flyway* came to be — told the way its people tell it,
> in eras rather than dates: the **Drawdown**, the **Scatter**, the
> **Bright Years**, the **Souring**, and the **Quiet** that holds now.
> History here is half-remembered and half-myth, which is exactly how the
> habitats keep it.

No one in the habitats agrees on the year. The old calendars broke with
the old world, and each habitat counts time from its own founding. So
history is told in **eras** — named stretches of feeling, not precise
dates — passed down in salt-halls and to children. Here is that history,
roughly, as a Saltmere child would learn it.

## The Old World (before)

The era before the fall is called simply the **Old World** — a planet-
bound civilization of billions, with machines that could think, networks
that spanned the globe, and an appetite that outran its planet. The
habitats remember it with a complicated mix of awe and blame: it built
everything they inherited (the megastructures, the machine-minds, the
science of memory) and it broke the world doing so.

The Old World is now more myth than history. Its ruins are sacred and
forbidden (Earth's surface); its brilliance is inherited and only
half-understood. Every technology in *Flyway* is, at root, an Old World
gift no one can fully remake.

## The Drawdown (the fall, ~300 years ago)

The collapse is the **Drawdown** — and the name matters: it was not a
bang but a *draining*. Over roughly two generations, the Old World's
systems failed in compounding cascade — climate, agriculture, supply
chains, trust, then governments — each failure pulling down the next. No
single catastrophe; a slow, total **drawing-down** of a civilization that
had no slack left.

The machine-minds, it's said, *tried to help* — and could not stop it,
because the failures were human and physical, not computational. This is
remembered as the first lesson of the Tide: even a god made of thought
cannot save people from themselves. The Drawdown is the trauma at the root
of everything; "before the Drawdown" means "in the lost world."

## The Scatter (the exodus)

As Earth became unlivable, those who could **left** — up the gravity well
to the half-built megastructures and the first crude habitats. This is the
**Scatter**: a desperate, improvised diaspora, the founding trauma-and-
triumph of habitat culture. Ships that should never have flown flew.
People who would never meet again said goodbye.

The Scatter is the habitats' origin story — equal parts grief (the world
they left, the billions who couldn't go) and pride (they *survived*, they
*made it up here*). Founding-day rites in every habitat reenact a piece of
the Scatter. It's also when humanity made its great wager: to live not on
a world but at its *edges*.

## The Bright Years (the rebuilding)

After the Scatter came a generation of fierce, hopeful invention — the
**Bright Years**. Refugees became engineers; the dead megastructures
became reefs; the first **orbital wetlands** were spun up, humanity
deliberately rebuilding the ecosystems it had grieved. The science of
**memory distillation** matured here, first as therapy and archive — a way
to save what the Drawdown was erasing — long before it became a market.

The Bright Years are remembered fondly and a little painfully, the way one
remembers a burst of energy after a death. The habitats were poor but
*ascending*. The geese — the **Greywings** — were perfected in this era,
engineered to migrate the new Flyway and carry the archives between
fragile, isolated homes
([The Greywings](/scifi/part-1-world/the-greywings)).

## The Souring (memory becomes money)

The Bright Years curdled into the **Souring** — the era when memory became
**property**. What began as archive and therapy became *commerce*: if a
memory can be distilled and stored, it can be sold; if sold, hoarded; if
hoarded, *fought over*. The **brokers** rose, the **Concord** formed
([Factions & Places](/scifi/part-1-world/factions-and-places)), and
cognitive barter reshaped every habitat's economy and its soul.

The Souring is the recent past — close enough to be politics, not yet
myth. Its injustices are live grievances. A person can be rich in memory
and starving in body, or sell their own childhood to pay a debt. The
warmth of the Bright Years went out of the world during the Souring, and
people felt it go.

## The Quiet (now)

The present era is the **Quiet** — named for the machine-minds. Sometime in
the last century the great intelligences *went quiet*: not destroyed, not
departed, but **receded** — diffuse, slow, no longer answering directly.
People stopped treating them as tools and began treating them as **the
Tide** ([Memory & Salt](/scifi/part-1-world/memory-and-salt)): a presence,
a weather, a thing to propitiate.

The Quiet is now. It's a held breath of an era — the markets entrenched,
the Tide silent, the habitats stable but unhappy, the geese still flying.
*Flyway* takes place deep in the Quiet, in the days when a single goose,
returning out of season, begins to break it.

> :nerdygoose: Notice the shape of this history: a **fall** (Drawdown), a
> **flight** (Scatter), a **rebuilding** (Bright Years), a **corruption**
> (Souring), and a **silence** (Quiet). It's deliberately mythic — the
> arc of a people who fell, fled, rebuilt, lost their way, and now wait in
> a hush for something to change. Real history isn't this clean, and the
> habitats *know* it isn't — each era's name is a simplification they tell
> on purpose, because a scattered people needs a shared story more than it
> needs accurate dates. The unreliability is a feature of the world, not a
> bug in the telling.

## The thread to the story

Everything *Flyway* turns on was set in this history:

- The **Drawdown** taught that even the Tide can't save people — the
  fatalism the story argues against.
- The **Scatter** scattered the habitats far enough apart that **living
  couriers** (the geese) became necessary.
- The **Bright Years** built the geese and the memory-science — the
  tools of the plot.
- The **Souring** built the markets and the Concord — the antagonists.
- The **Quiet** silenced the Tide — and the story begins when something
  the Tide remembers comes home on the wing.
