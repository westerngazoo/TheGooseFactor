# Physics Basics

Physics through the lens of computation — formulas as reusable building blocks, conservation laws as invariants, dimensional analysis as a debugging tool.

## What's Covered

### Coming Soon

- **Kinematics & dynamics**: F=ma discretized, projectile motion, harmonic oscillator
- **Energy & momentum**: conservation as invariants, problem framing with energy methods
- **Dimensional analysis**: the physicist's type checker — catch errors before you compute
- **Computational physics**: time stepping, numerical stability, Euler vs Runge-Kutta
- **Statistical mechanics analogies**: simulated annealing, random walks, diffusion as search
- **Optimization landscapes**: potential energy surfaces, gradient flows, saddle points

## Approach

- **Formula first, intuition second**: state the result, then explain why it's true.
- **Computational framing**: every physics concept connects to a numerical method or algorithm.
- **Units as types**: dimensional analysis catches bugs the way a type system catches them in code.
- **Goose personas** argue about approximations so you know when to trust them.
