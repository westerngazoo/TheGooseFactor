## How our book differs

This book is a **complement** to D-L, not a replacement. The
differences:

- **Voice**: D-L is a textbook in tone; we're informal, with
  opinion-laden goose admonitions.
- **Scope**: D-L is comprehensive; we hit the headlines and
  cross-reference back for details.
- **Audience**: D-L assumes physics-grad maturity from page one;
  we try to make Parts I-III accessible to a math-prepared
  undergraduate.
- **Coverage**: D-L has more exercises, more detailed derivations,
  more advanced topics (e.g., chiral spinors in detail, more on
  GTG). We're aiming for the working physicist who wants a
  navigable summary.

Use both. D-L is the depth; this book is the breadth and the
voice.
