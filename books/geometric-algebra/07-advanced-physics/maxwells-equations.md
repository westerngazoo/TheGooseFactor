# Maxwell's Equations

One of the most striking successes of Geometric Algebra, highlighted by researchers like Doran and Lasenby, is the simplification of electromagnetism.

Using the spacetime derivative $\nabla$ and the electromagnetic bivector field $F$, the traditional four Maxwell equations (along with their complicated vector calculus formulations) reduce to a single, elegant equation:

$$ \nabla F = J $$

This single equation contains all the physics of electromagnetism, capturing both the propagation of waves and the interaction with charges and currents seamlessly.
