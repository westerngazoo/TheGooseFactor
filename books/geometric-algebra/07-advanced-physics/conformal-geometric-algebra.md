# Conformal Geometric Algebra (CGA)

Conformal Geometric Algebra expands the typical Euclidean space to represent points, lines, circles, planes, and spheres as fundamental algebraic entities.

By adding two additional dimensions (one representing the origin, and one representing the point at infinity), CGA allows Euclidean transformations like translations and rotations to be handled universally as rotors. This framework, extensively developed by Hestenes and later by Doran and others, is a powerful tool for computer graphics, robotics, and advanced physics.
