## What it covers

Duality as the operation that maps a $k$-blade to its
$(n-k)$-blade orthogonal complement. The video uses the
**Hodge star** notation $\star$ and walks through how multiplying
by the pseudoscalar $I$ implements it:

$$\star \mathbf{A} = \mathbf{A} \cdot I^{-1}$$

The Hodge star is what differential-geometry texts call duality.
GA shows it's the same operation as multiplication by the
pseudoscalar.

## Why this video is worth watching

Differential geometers, physicists, and GA practitioners all use
the same operation under different names. The video explicitly
identifies them:

- "Hodge star" in differential forms.
- "Multiplication by the pseudoscalar" in GA.
- "Orthogonal complement" in linear algebra.
- "Dual" in projective geometry.

Knowing the four names are the same thing saves a lot of mental
overhead when you read across literatures.

> :nerdygoose: Same operation, four notations. Welcome to math —
> where the worst friction in cross-field reading is just
> recognizing that the new symbol means something you already
> know.
