# Video Companion — Mathoma's Geometric Algebra Series

This section is a one-page companion per episode of
[Mathoma's Geometric Algebra playlist on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLffJUy1BnWj3deu0cqpk5CiePiwORDiCe).
Ten videos, ten short summaries, each cross-referencing to the
parts of this book that cover the same material.

## Why a companion section?

Two reasons:

1. **Different teaching style.** The book is a written study
   journal — dense, persona-driven, and assumes you'll read at
   your own pace. The videos are visual, paced, and excellent
   for first exposure. They complement each other.
2. **Cross-reference for the curious.** If something in the book
   feels abstract, watching the corresponding video usually
   un-abstracts it. If something in a video feels rushed, the
   book chapter has the slow-motion version.

> :happygoose: Pedagogy is plural. Read the book *and* watch the
> videos — different lobes of your brain light up. The redundancy
> is the point.

## How to use

- Pick an episode that matches what you're trying to learn.
- Watch the video first if you're new to the topic.
- Read the corresponding book chapter for the slower, more
  rigorous treatment.
- Use the **"In this book"** sidebar of each companion page to
  jump to the right book chapter.

> :weightliftinggoose: One pass through the videos, one pass
> through the book, and you've trained the same idea two
> different ways. That's how concepts stick.
