## What it covers

The motivation. Why GA is worth learning, what it unifies (dot
product + cross product + complex numbers + quaternions), and
where each of those tools breaks. Mathoma's framing is "you've
been given a fragmented mess; this is the unification."

## In this book

- [Why Geometric Algebra (intro)](/geometric-algebra) — the book's
  own front-door pitch, in roughly the same spirit but written
  rather than spoken.
- [Vectors and the Geometric Product](/geometric-algebra/01-foundations/vectors-and-the-geometric-product)
  — picks up where the video stops, deriving the fundamental
  identity from two axioms.

## Reading suggestion

This video is the right thing to watch first if GA is brand new
to you. The book opens with the same motivation in writing — read
it as the second pass, then watch the video again with the
written framing in mind.

> :happygoose: First exposure: video. Second exposure: book.
> Third exposure: do an exercise. That's the loop.
