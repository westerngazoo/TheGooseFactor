## What it covers

A re-derivation of standard linear-algebra results — determinants,
volumes of parallelepipeds, linear independence — using the wedge
product as the primitive operation instead of the determinant.

Key insight: $\mathbf{a} \wedge \mathbf{b} \wedge \mathbf{c}$ is
zero exactly when the three vectors are linearly dependent. The
wedge *is* the linear-independence detector. Determinants are a
specific scalar coordinate of the wedge.

The episode walks through Cramer's rule, volume computations, and
the subspace test, all in wedge language.

## What this video gives you that the book doesn't

A focused, video-paced re-grounding of "everything you know about
determinants, restated in wedge language." If you've taken linear
algebra and felt that Cramer's rule was a magic formula, this
episode cleans it up.

The book covers the underlying machinery but doesn't dedicate a
chapter to "here's how the standard LA results re-derive" —
that's exactly what the video does well.
