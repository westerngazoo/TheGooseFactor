## What it covers

The reveal: complex numbers are the *even subalgebra* of
$\text{Cl}(2)$. The "imaginary unit" $i$ is the real, geometric
bivector $\mathbf{e}_{12}$. There's nothing imaginary about it —
it's the unit oriented plane.

The video walks through the identification:

$$z = a + bi \longleftrightarrow Z = a + b\mathbf{e}_{12}$$

shows the multiplication rules match, and points out that this
is *not* an analogy but an isomorphism.

## The takeaway

If you've used complex numbers — which most STEM majors have —
you've already used GA. You just didn't know what the framework
looked like. The video makes this concrete with computation; the
book extends it to "and here's what the analog is in 3D
(quaternions) and 4D and beyond."

> :surprisedgoose: 250 years of "imaginary numbers." All it took
> was renaming $i$ to $\mathbf{e}_{12}$ and the imaginary becomes
> geometric. Mathematicians knew this in the 19th century. The
> nomenclature is just slow to update.
