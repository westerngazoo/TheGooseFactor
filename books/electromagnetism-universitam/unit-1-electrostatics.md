# Unit I: Electrostatics

Electrostatics studies charges at rest and the fields they produce. It is the gentlest entry point into Geometric Algebra E&M because the field is a pure **vector** field here — the bivector (magnetic) part only appears once charges move. We use this unit to introduce the vector derivative $\nabla$ and to recast Gauss's law as a statement about $\nabla\cdot\mathbf{E}$, setting up the unification to come.

## Planned Sections

- Electric charge and Coulomb's law.
- The electric field $\mathbf{E}$ as force per unit charge.
- Gauss's law in integral and differential ($\nabla\cdot\mathbf{E} = \rho/\varepsilon_0$) form.
- Electric potential, work, and energy.
- Conductors and boundary conditions.
