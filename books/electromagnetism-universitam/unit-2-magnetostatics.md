# Unit II: Magnetostatics

Magnetostatics studies the fields produced by steady currents. This is where the standard treatment first stumbles: it calls $\mathbf{B}$ a vector, yet $\mathbf{B}$ behaves like an *area* — it flips sign under reflection and is born from a cross product. Geometric Algebra resolves the discomfort by identifying the magnetic field as a **bivector**, $I\mathbf{B}$, an oriented plane rather than an arrow.
