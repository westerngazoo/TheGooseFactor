# Electromagnetism Universitam: A Geometric Algebra Approach

Welcome to **Electromagnetism Universitam**, an alternate study of electromagnetism reimagined through the lens of **Geometric Algebra (GA)**.

The traditional presentation gives you **four** Maxwell equations, written with two different fields ($\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{B}$), two different vector operators (divergence and curl), and a cross product that obscures the fact that $\mathbf{B}$ is not really a vector at all. Geometric Algebra reveals the truth: there is **one** electromagnetic field $F$ — a bivector built from $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ — and **one** equation,

$$ \nabla F = J, $$

that contains all four of Maxwell's laws at once. This is the destination of the course, and everything we build is aimed at earning it honestly.

## Why This Approach

- **One field.** The magnetic field is the *spatial bivector* part of $F$; combining $\mathbf{E}$ and $\mathbf{B}$ into $F = \mathbf{E} + I c\mathbf{B}$ (in 3D GA) explains why they mix under boosts.
- **One operator.** The vector derivative $\nabla$ replaces separate div and curl — they are the scalar and bivector parts of $\nabla F$.
- **One equation.** In spacetime algebra, $\nabla F = J$ is Maxwell's equations. Charge conservation $\nabla\cdot J = 0$ follows in one line.

## What to Expect in This Course

We follow a standard E&M syllabus — electrostatics, magnetostatics, induction, Maxwell, waves — but each piece is a step toward the unified picture:

- **Electrostatics** introduces the field and the role of the vector derivative.
- **Magnetostatics** shows why the "magnetic field vector" is better understood as a bivector.
- **Induction** brings in time and the coupling of the fields.
- **The unification** assembles $F$ and writes $\nabla F = J$.
- **Waves** fall out as source-free solutions $\nabla F = 0$.

> :mathgoose: The invariant to keep in view: $\nabla\cdot J = \nabla\cdot(\nabla F) = 0$ identically, because the divergence of a derivative of this form vanishes. Charge conservation is not an extra postulate — it is a theorem baked into the structure of $\nabla F = J$.

## The Units

We have mapped the traditional electromagnetism syllabus into a GA-first framework:

1. **Unit I: Electrostatics** — Charge, the electric field, Gauss's law, and potential, with the vector derivative introduced as the natural language.
2. **Unit II: Magnetostatics** — Steady currents, the magnetic field as a bivector, and Ampère's law without the cross-product baggage.
3. **Unit III: Electromagnetic Induction** — Faraday's law, time-varying fields, and the coupling of electric and magnetic parts.
4. **Unit IV: Maxwell Unified in GA** — Assembling the field $F$ and collapsing all four equations into $\nabla F = J$ via spacetime algebra.
5. **Unit V: Electromagnetic Waves** — Source-free solutions $\nabla F = 0$, polarization, and energy/momentum of the field.
