# Table of Contents

Electromagnetism Universitam follows a standard E&M arc — electrostatics, magnetostatics,
induction, Maxwell, waves — building toward the single spacetime-algebra equation
$\nabla F = J$. Below is the planned structure. (Use the sidebar to navigate.)

## Unit I — Electrostatics
Charge and Coulomb's law, the electric field, Gauss's law, electric potential and energy,
and conductors. The vector derivative $\nabla$ is introduced as the unifying operator.

## Unit II — Magnetostatics
Steady currents, the magnetic field reinterpreted as a spatial bivector, the Biot–Savart
and Ampère laws, and why the usual "magnetic field vector" is an axial-vector artifact of
the cross product.

## Unit III — Electromagnetic Induction
Faraday's law of induction, motional EMF, time-varying fields, inductance, and the first
explicit coupling between the electric and magnetic parts of the field.

## Unit IV — Maxwell Unified in GA
Assembling the electromagnetic field $F = \mathbf{E} + Ic\mathbf{B}$, the spacetime vector
derivative, and the collapse of all four Maxwell equations into the single equation
$\nabla F = J$ — with charge conservation as an immediate corollary.

## Unit V — Electromagnetic Waves
Source-free solutions $\nabla F = 0$, plane waves and polarization, the wave equation,
and the energy–momentum content of the electromagnetic field.
