# Appendix C — Glossary

Quick definitions of the terms used throughout the book. GA terms,
ML terms, and the bridge vocabulary.

## Geometric algebra terms

**Blade** — a multivector formed as the wedge of $k$ vectors;
represents an oriented $k$-dimensional subspace. A 1-blade is a
vector, a 2-blade a plane element, etc.

**Bivector** — a grade-2 multivector. Represents an oriented plane /
rotation. In GA, the Lie algebra of the rotation group; the angular-
velocity, angular-momentum, and electromagnetic-field objects.

**Clifford algebra** — the algebra generated by vectors under the
geometric product with $e_i^2 = \pm 1$, $e_i e_j = -e_j e_i$. Denoted
$\mathcal{Cl}(p,q)$ for $p$ positive and $q$ negative squares.

**Conformal GA (CGA)** — the algebra $\mathcal{Cl}(n+1,1)$ that embeds
$\mathbb{R}^n$ so that translations, rotations, dilations become
rotors. The home of robotics motors and geometric primitives.

**Geometric product** — the fundamental GA operation. For vectors,
$ab = a\cdot b + a\wedge b$ (scalar + bivector). Combines inner and
outer products; does Clebsch-Gordan decomposition.

**Grade** — the "dimension level" of a multivector component: grade 0
(scalar), 1 (vector), 2 (bivector), 3 (trivector/pseudoscalar in 3D).
Carries group representations in equivariant networks.

**Motor** — a conformal-GA rotor representing a rigid motion (screw
motion = rotation + translation). The robotics workhorse.

**Multivector** — a general element of a Clifford algebra; a sum of
components of different grades.

**Outer product (wedge, ∧)** — the antisymmetric part of the
geometric product. Builds higher-grade blades. $a\wedge a = 0$.

**Pseudoscalar (I)** — the highest-grade blade (grade $n$ in $n$-D).
Implements duality; in 3D, $I^2 = -1$ acts as the imaginary unit. The
chirality detector (flips under reflection).

**Rotor** — an even-grade unit multivector, $R = \exp(B/2)$, that
implements rotations via the sandwich $RvR^{-1}$. In 3D, isomorphic to
unit quaternions.

**Sandwich product** — the operation $RvR^{-1}$ (or $RvR̃$ for unit
rotors) that applies a rotor to a multivector. Preserves grades; the
group-action representation in equivariant GA.

**Twist** — an $SE(3)$ velocity bivector, combining linear and
angular velocity into one object.

## Machine learning terms

**Clebsch-Gordan (CG)** — the rule for decomposing tensor products of
group representations into irreducibles. In GA, performed implicitly
by the geometric product.

**Equivariance** — $f(\rho(g)x) = \rho'(g)f(x)$: transforming the
input produces a correspondingly-transformed output. The central
property of geometric networks.

**Invariance** — $f(\rho(g)x) = f(x)$: the output is unchanged by the
group action. A special case of equivariance (trivial output
representation).

**Irreducible representation (irrep)** — a group representation with
no nontrivial invariant subspace; the "atoms" of representation
theory. For $SO(3)$, the "type-$\ell$" $(2\ell+1)$-dimensional
representations.

**Message passing** — the GNN computation pattern: compute messages on
edges, aggregate at nodes, update node states.

**Steerable** — features/networks whose response transforms
predictably (by a representation) under group actions; synonymous with
equivariant in the irrep literature.

## Bridge vocabulary (GA ↔ ML/physics)

| GA term | ML/physics equivalent |
|---|---|
| Grade | Tensor rank / irrep type |
| Bivector | Lie algebra element / angular quantity |
| Rotor | Group element / rotation operator |
| Geometric product | Clebsch-Gordan tensor product |
| Sandwich | Representation action |
| Pseudoscalar | Chirality / signed volume / Hodge dual |
| Motor | $SE(3)$ element / rigid transform |
| Exponential map | Lie algebra → Lie group |

## Group theory terms

**Lie group** — a continuous (smooth-manifold) group. $SO(3)$, $SE(3)$.

**Lie algebra** — the tangent space at the identity of a Lie group;
infinitesimal generators. In GA, the bivectors.

**$SO(3)$** — 3D rotations. $SE(3)$ — 3D rigid motions (rotation +
translation). $E(n)$ — Euclidean group (rotations, reflections,
translations). $S_n$ — permutations.

**$\mathrm{Spin}(n)$ / $\mathrm{Pin}(n)$** — the double covers of
$SO(n)$ / $O(n)$; the rotor / reflection groups in GA.

**Exponential map** — $\exp : \mathfrak{g} \to G$, taking a Lie-algebra
element to a group element. In GA, $\exp(B/2) = R$.

## Architecture names

**EGNN** — E(n)-Equivariant Graph Neural Network (Satorras et al.).
Scalars + vectors, the simplest equivariant architecture.

**SE(3)-Transformer** — equivariant attention with full irrep features
(Fuchs et al.).

**Clifford layers / GCAN** — GA-native architectures using multivector
features and geometric-product operations (Brandstetter; Ruhe et al.).

**Equiformer** — equivariant transformer for atomistic systems (Liao &
Smidt).

**NequIP / MACE / Allegro** — state-of-the-art equivariant ML force
fields for molecular dynamics.

**gafro** — Geometric Algebra For RObotics; the C++ robotics library.

**RoPE** — Rotary Positional Embedding; rotor-based positional
encoding used in modern LLMs.
