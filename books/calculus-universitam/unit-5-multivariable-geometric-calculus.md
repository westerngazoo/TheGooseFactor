# Unit V: Multivariable & Geometric Calculus

This is the payoff unit. We introduce the **vector derivative** $\nabla$ and show that gradient, divergence, and curl are nothing more than the scalar and bivector parts of the single geometric product $\nabla F$. Directed integration over curves, surfaces, and volumes then collapses Green's theorem, the divergence theorem, and Stokes' theorem into one **fundamental theorem of geometric calculus**.

## Planned Sections

- Partial derivatives and the multivariable chain rule.
- The vector derivative $\nabla$ and the geometric product $\nabla F = \nabla\cdot F + \nabla\wedge F$.
- Gradient, divergence, and curl as projections of $\nabla F$ (no cross product required).
- Directed integration over curves, surfaces, and volumes.
- The Fundamental Theorem of Geometric Calculus: $\int_R \nabla F\, |dV| = \oint_{\partial R} F\, |dS|$, and how Green/Gauss/Stokes are special cases.
