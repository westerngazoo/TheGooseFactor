# Table of Contents

Calculus Universitam follows the standard single- and multivariable calculus arc, but
every topic is reframed through Geometric Algebra. Below is the planned structure of the
course. (This is a prose map of the units — use the sidebar to navigate.)

## Unit I — Limits & Continuity
The rigorous foundation. Neighborhoods and the idea of "approach," the $\varepsilon$–$\delta$
definition, limit laws, one-sided and infinite limits, and continuity for scalar- and
vector-valued maps. This is the fully written exemplar unit and the quality bar for the
rest of the course.

## Unit II — The Derivative as a Linear Map
The derivative reframed as the best local linear approximation. Difference quotients,
differentiability versus continuity, the directional derivative, and the leap to the
vector derivative. Product, chain, and quotient rules recovered as statements about
linear maps.

## Unit III — Directed Integration
Integration as the summation of *oriented* quantities. Riemann sums with a directed
measure, the fundamental theorem of calculus read as a boundary statement, techniques of
integration, and the first hint of why $d\mathbf{x}$ should be a vector.

## Unit IV — Sequences & Series
Convergence of sequences and series, tests for convergence, power series and radius of
convergence, and Taylor/Maclaurin expansions — including expansions of multivector-valued
functions such as the exponential of a bivector (rotors).

## Unit V — Multivariable & Geometric Calculus
The vector derivative $\nabla$ and the geometric product. How gradient, divergence, and
curl are the scalar and bivector parts of $\nabla F$. Directed integration over curves,
surfaces, and volumes, culminating in the fundamental theorem of geometric calculus that
contains Green's, Gauss's, and Stokes's theorems as special cases.
