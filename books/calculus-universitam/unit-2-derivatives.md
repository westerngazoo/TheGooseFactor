# Unit II: The Derivative as a Linear Map

In Geometric Algebra the derivative is not "the slope of the tangent line" — it is the **best local linear approximation** of a map. Reframing differentiation as a linear map (rather than a single number) is what lets the same idea scale from $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ all the way up to the vector derivative of Unit V. This unit develops that viewpoint and recovers every familiar differentiation rule as a statement about linear maps.

## Planned Sections

- The derivative as the best linear approximation: $f(a+h) = f(a) + f'(a)\,h + o(h)$.
- Rules of differentiation (product, quotient, chain) derived from linearity.
- The directional derivative and the leap toward the vector derivative $\nabla$.
