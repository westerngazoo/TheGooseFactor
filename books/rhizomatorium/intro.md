# Rhizomatorium: A Geometric Algebra Approach

Welcome to **Rhizomatorium**, an alternate study of physics reimagined through the lens of **Geometric Algebra (GA)**. 

Traditional physics courses heavily rely on vector calculus and linear algebra. While powerful, standard vector algebra often introduces artificial constructs like the cross product (which only strictly works in 3 dimensions) and requires separate, disconnected rules for rotations, projections, and pseudo-vectors.

Geometric Algebra unifies these mathematical descriptions. By treating points, lines, areas, and volumes as fundamental, interoperable objects, GA simplifies physical laws to their most elegant forms.

## What to Expect in This Course
In this alternate study, we revisit classical physics topics by upgrading our mathematical toolkit. Instead of relying solely on standard vectors, we will utilize:
- **Vectors** (1D lines) for translation and forces.
- **Bivectors** (2D areas) for rotations, angular momentum, and torque.
- **Rotors** to elegantly handle continuous rotations without the pitfalls of Euler angles or standard matrices.

### The Units
We have mapped the traditional Physics syllabus into a GA-first framework:
1. **Unit I: Kinematics** - Motion described without cross products. Velocity and acceleration defined geometrically.
2. **Unit II: Dynamics (Newton's Laws)** - Force, mass, and the conservation of momentum expressed in the unified language of multivectors.
3. **Unit III: Gravitation** - Re-evaluating the gravitational field through the geometric product and spatial gradients.
4. **Unit IV: Kepler's Laws & Free Fall** - Utilizing the area-preserving nature of bivectors to elegantly derive Kepler's second law (conservation of angular momentum).

Let's embark on this journey to see how the universe's mechanics are intimately tied to its underlying geometric structure.
