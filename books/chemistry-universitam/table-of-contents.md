# Table of Contents

Chemistry Universitam follows a standard general-chemistry arc — atomic structure,
periodic trends, bonding, stoichiometry, thermochemistry — taught rigorously with a
geometric sensibility for shape and symmetry. Below is the planned structure. (Use the
sidebar to navigate.)

## Unit I — Atomic Structure
Subatomic particles, the nuclear and quantum models of the atom, quantum numbers, electron
configuration, and the angular shapes of $s$, $p$, and $d$ orbitals.

## Unit II — Periodic Trends
The periodic table as organized structure: periodicity, atomic and ionic radius, ionization
energy, electron affinity, and electronegativity, all read as consequences of atomic
structure.

## Unit III — Bonding & Molecular Geometry
Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding, Lewis structures, VSEPR-predicted geometry,
hybridization, and molecular point-group symmetry — the most geometric unit of the course.

## Unit IV — Stoichiometry & Reactions
The mole concept, molar mass, balancing chemical equations, limiting reagents and yield,
and the major classes of reaction.

## Unit V — Thermochemistry
Energy and heat, enthalpy, calorimetry, Hess's law, standard enthalpies of formation, and
the energetics that drive chemical change.
