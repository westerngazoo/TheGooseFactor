# Unit V: Thermochemistry

Thermochemistry is energy accounting for chemical change: how much heat a reaction absorbs or releases, and why. Like stoichiometry, it rests on a conservation law — energy — and on state functions whose path-independence (Hess's law) is a genuinely structural fact, not a convenience.
