# Unit IV: Stoichiometry & Reactions

Stoichiometry is the quantitative bookkeeping of chemistry: counting atoms through a reaction so that nothing is created or destroyed. This unit is deliberately numerical and disciplined — the conservation laws here are as strict as any invariant in the math courses, and we hold them to the same standard.

## Planned Sections

- The mole concept and Avogadro's number.
- Molar mass and percent composition.
- Balancing chemical equations as conservation of atoms.
- Limiting reagents, theoretical and percent yield.
- Classes of reaction: synthesis, decomposition, single/double replacement, combustion, redox.
- Worked examples and persona callouts matching the Calculus Universitam Unit I quality bar.
