# Unit II: Periodic Trends

The periodic table is not a lookup chart — it is **organized structure**. Once the orbital picture of Unit I is in hand, the great trends (size, ionization energy, electronegativity) become predictable consequences of how electrons fill shells and how strongly the nucleus holds them. This unit turns the table into a reasoning tool.

## Planned Sections

- The structure of the periodic table: periods, groups, and blocks.
- Effective nuclear charge and shielding.
- Atomic and ionic radius trends.
- Ionization energy and electron affinity.
- Electronegativity and its role in predicting bonding.
