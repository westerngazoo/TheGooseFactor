# Chemistry Universitam: A Geometric Approach

Welcome to **Chemistry Universitam**, an alternate study of general chemistry that keeps the rigor and house style of the Universitam program while leaning into the subject's deeply **geometric** heart.

Chemistry is, at bottom, a science of *shape and symmetry*. Orbitals are angular functions; bonds have angles; molecules have point-group symmetries; reactions are reorganizations of geometry and charge. We keep the standard, experimentally grounded chemistry curriculum, and where geometry naturally appears — orbital shapes, molecular geometry (VSEPR), symmetry operations — we describe it in the language of oriented quantities and transformations familiar from the rest of Universitam. The Geometric Algebra tie-in here is **lighter** than in the math and physics courses, by design: chemistry is taught honestly, with geometry as the connective tissue rather than a forced reformulation.

## Why This Approach

- **Symmetry as transformation.** Reflections, rotations, and inversions — the operations of molecular point groups — are exactly the orthogonal maps GA describes as products of reflections.
- **Orbitals are geometry.** The shapes of $s$, $p$, and $d$ orbitals are angular structures; thinking of them as oriented objects clarifies bonding and hybridization.
- **Quantitative discipline.** Stoichiometry, thermochemistry, and equilibrium are kept rigorous and numerical, in the same formula-forward voice as the rest of the program.

## What to Expect in This Course

A standard general-chemistry syllabus, taught carefully, with a geometric sensibility:

- **Atomic structure** and the quantum numbers that index orbital geometry.
- **Periodic trends** as consequences of structure.
- **Bonding and molecular geometry**, where symmetry and shape take center stage.
- **Stoichiometry and reactions**, the quantitative bookkeeping of chemistry.
- **Thermochemistry**, energy accounting in chemical change.

> :happygoose: Don't expect every reaction to be rewritten as a bivector — that would be forcing it. Expect instead that when *shape* matters (orbitals, VSEPR, symmetry), we'll reach for the clean geometric language the rest of Universitam already taught you.

## The Units

We have mapped a standard general-chemistry syllabus into the Universitam house style:

1. **Unit I: Atomic Structure** — Subatomic particles, the quantum model, quantum numbers, and orbital shapes.
2. **Unit II: Periodic Trends** — The periodic table as organized structure: atomic radius, ionization energy, electronegativity.
3. **Unit III: Bonding & Molecular Geometry** — Ionic and covalent bonds, Lewis structures, VSEPR, hybridization, and molecular symmetry.
4. **Unit IV: Stoichiometry & Reactions** — The mole, balancing equations, limiting reagents, and reaction types.
5. **Unit V: Thermochemistry** — Energy, enthalpy, Hess's law, and the energetics of reactions.
