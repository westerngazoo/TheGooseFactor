# Unit III: Bonding & Molecular Geometry

This is the most geometric unit of the course. Bonds have lengths and angles; molecules have shapes; and those shapes obey **symmetry**. Reflections, rotations, and inversions — the operations of a molecular point group — are precisely the orthogonal transformations the rest of Universitam describes as products of reflections. Here is where the program's geometric language earns its keep in chemistry.

## Planned Sections

- Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding.
- Lewis structures and formal charge.
- VSEPR theory and predicted molecular geometries.
- Hybridization ($sp$, $sp^2$, $sp^3$) and orbital overlap.
- Molecular symmetry and point groups as products of reflections/rotations.
