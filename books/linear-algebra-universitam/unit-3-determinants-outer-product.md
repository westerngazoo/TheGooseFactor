# Unit III: Determinants & the Outer Product

The determinant is usually presented as an alarming alternating sum. In Geometric Algebra it is the most natural object in the world: the **oriented volume** $\mathbf{a}_1 \wedge \mathbf{a}_2 \wedge \cdots \wedge \mathbf{a}_n$, expressed as a multiple of the unit pseudoscalar. The antisymmetry of the wedge ($\mathbf{a}\wedge\mathbf{b} = -\,\mathbf{b}\wedge\mathbf{a}$) *is* the sign rule, and a determinant of a linear map is simply the factor by which it scales that volume.
