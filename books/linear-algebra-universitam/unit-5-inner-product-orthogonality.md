# Unit V: Inner Products & Orthogonality

The inner product measures projection and length; orthogonality is its vanishing. In Geometric Algebra the inner product is the grade-lowering half of the geometric product ($\mathbf{u}\cdot\mathbf{v} = \tfrac12(\mathbf{u}\mathbf{v} + \mathbf{v}\mathbf{u})$), and the great structural theorem of this unit is that **every orthogonal transformation is a product of reflections** — with rotations (rotors) being the even-numbered case.
