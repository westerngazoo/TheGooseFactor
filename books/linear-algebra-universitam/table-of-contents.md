# Table of Contents

Linear Algebra Universitam follows a standard first-course arc — spaces, maps,
determinants, eigenvalues, inner products — but reframes each topic through the outer
product and the geometric product. Below is the planned structure. (Use the sidebar to
navigate.)

## Unit I — Vectors & Spaces
Vector spaces and subspaces, span and linear combinations, linear independence
characterized by the outer product ($\mathbf{a}\wedge\mathbf{b}\neq 0$), basis, and
dimension.

## Unit II — Linear Maps & Matrices
Linear transformations and their properties, the matrix as a basis-dependent
representation of a basis-free map, composition, kernel and image, and the rank–nullity
theorem.

## Unit III — Determinants & the Outer Product
The determinant introduced as the scalar magnitude of $\mathbf{a}_1\wedge\cdots\wedge\mathbf{a}_n$
— oriented-volume scaling — recovering cofactor and multiplicative properties, with rank
and invertibility as the vanishing or non-vanishing of a wedge.

## Unit IV — Eigenvalues & Eigenstuff
Eigenvalues and eigenvectors as invariant directions, eigenplanes (bivectors) for
rotational maps, characteristic polynomials, diagonalization, and a geometric reading of
complex eigenvalues.

## Unit V — Inner Products & Orthogonality
The inner product and norms, orthogonality and projection, Gram–Schmidt and orthonormal
bases, and orthogonal transformations expressed as reflections and rotors.
