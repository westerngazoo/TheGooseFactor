# Unit II: Linear Maps & Matrices

A linear map is a transformation that respects addition and scaling. The matrix you write down for it is only its **shadow in a chosen basis** — change the basis and the numbers change, while the map does not. Geometric Algebra keeps this distinction front and center, and (in later units) extends a linear map from vectors to bivectors and beyond via the outermorphism.
