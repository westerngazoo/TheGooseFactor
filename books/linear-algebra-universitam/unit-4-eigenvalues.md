# Unit IV: Eigenvalues & Eigenstuff

Eigenvalues identify the **invariant geometry** of a linear map: directions that are merely stretched, and planes that are merely rotated. Geometric Algebra gives an especially clean reading of the "complex eigenvalues" that puzzle students in the standard treatment — they signal an invariant **plane** (a bivector) on which the map acts as a rotation, no imaginary numbers required.
