# Universitam

**Universitam** is a university curriculum reimagined from the ground up through the
unifying language of **Geometric Algebra (GA)**.

Traditional STEM courses each invent their own notation: cross products in physics,
determinants in linear algebra, curl and divergence in electromagnetism, gradient
operators in calculus. These tools work, but they fragment the same underlying
geometric ideas into a dozen incompatible dialects. Universitam takes the opposite
bet: teach one algebra of oriented quantities — scalars, vectors, bivectors,
trivectors, and their geometric product — and let every subject fall out as a special
case.

The result is a degree-style program where the math you learn in one course is *exactly*
the math you reuse in the next. A bivector is an oriented area whether you meet it as
a wedge product in calculus, a 2-blade in linear algebra, the electromagnetic field
in Maxwell's equations, or angular momentum in mechanics.

## The Program

Each book below is a self-contained course. They share a common voice — a clean,
formula-forward university lecture — and a common cast of **goose personas** who heckle
the pitfalls, formalize the proofs, and celebrate the elegant results.

- [**Physics Universitam**](#) — Classical mechanics rebuilt without
  cross products: kinematics, dynamics, gravitation, and Kepler's laws expressed in
  multivectors and rotors.
- [**Calculus Universitam**](/calculus-universitam) — Limits, derivatives, and integration
  reframed as geometric operations, culminating in the GA vector derivative and directed
  integration.
- [**Linear Algebra Universitam**](/linear-algebra-universitam) — Vectors and bivectors,
  the outer product as the natural parent of the determinant, and linear maps as GA
  operations (rotors, reflections, outermorphisms).
- [**Electromagnetism Universitam**](/electromagnetism-universitam) — The four Maxwell
  equations collapsed into a single spacetime-algebra equation via the GA vector
  derivative.
- [**Chemistry Universitam**](/chemistry-universitam) — Atomic structure, periodic trends,
  bonding, and molecular geometry, with symmetry and orbital shape framed geometrically.

## How to Read These

You do not need to read them in order, but the GA toolkit compounds. If you are new to
Geometric Algebra, start with **Calculus Universitam** (which introduces the geometric
product gently) or the existing **Geometric Algebra** book on this site, then branch into
whichever subject you came for.

> Enter freely. Mind the bivectors.
